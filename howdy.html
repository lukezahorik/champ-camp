Parking Each car needs a pass for admission to the Arapaho National Recreation Area; fees are $5 a day, $10 for three days. Can be purchased at the campsite. Open gate and come on in and set up.

Champ Camp allows our friends and family to come together and meet one another. We’ll explore base camp on the shores of Lake Granby near the town of Grand Lake. This is an invitation to get out and enjoy God’s Great Creation with hiking, biking, fishing, kayaking, hot springing or just some relaxation and good outdoor fun. Spend 2 nights or as long as you can for one day.

ACTIVITIES: If you ain’t having fun … where’s your sense of adventure?

There will be a Mountain Man survival contest, all kinds of camper activities, treasure hunts, gold panning, rock hounding, stump stamping, bead bagging and mountain games to earn Champ Camp Cash. When the weather is fair, there will be kayaks to use and Champ will have hikes planned.

Bring something to trade (toy, stuffed animal, mug, your little brother) for your first bag of pay-dirt.

FOOD LOGISTICS: If the food is bad … the company better be good!

Well pilgrim, if you can’t catch it and cook it yourself then I hope you brought lots of jerky. Champ cooks good rabbit. But if you’ve grown pertic, ya better bring what you can stomach. What does that all mean? Kids if you’re hungry or thirsty, don’t bother mom! Ya better learn the #1 and most important Champ Camp rule – “ There ain’t no mommas in Champ Camp.” Mom is very very welcome of course, but kids you’re expected to fend for yourselves. No Wimps! There’s plenty of drinking water, but you little whipper snappers are gonna need to earn your keep by trading and panning for gold to earn Champ Camp Cash. Then you can buy snacks and such at the trading post.

Meals will be up to each individual Hunter (father). We don’t want the moms stuck cooking and cleaning. Teach your kids how to flip their own Flap Jacks and cook over an open fire (if no fire ban). Pack a lunch and take your family hiking. If you’re up early Champ will have "Gargle" ready; that’s Cowboy coffee, hot, black, and thick enough to float a six-shooter. Now, if you have any food that you don’t want to share, please mark it with your name and keep in your car in a cooler (bears). Otherwise, food is open game at this rendezvous. Champ will have a few cooking surfaces to use.

LODGING LOGISTICS: Like last year, All campers will be sleeping under the wondering stars so bring camping gear. We’re saving you a tent site at Cutthroat Bay Campground. It’s all paid for just show up and set up. Yes, all the ladies will be ruff’n it too this year (SORRY). Bring your own tent if you one, but I have 7 tents for use (so don’t buy one). But please let me know if you need one. Be prepared for frigid mornings, rain and cool nights. Waterproof your tents. Pack your saddle and bedroll (pillow, mats and sleeping bag). Plan to bathe at the hot springs or just enjoy your “mountain man” aroma. Yup fellas, there’s an outhouse (but it is really nice ladies). And ladies this ain’t no beauty pageant so we’d love to see you in a kayak, digging in the dirt and tossing some horseshoes with the kids.

If you would like use your own tin tipi (camper) please check into Green Ridge Campground that is located on Shadow Mountain Reservoir. If this sounds all too primitive for you, cabins and rooms are very nice in and around the Grand Lake area (but $$$). Have we lost any greenhorns yet?
